# Shopee Ads Dashboard üìä

This dashboard presents a comprehensive performance analysis of Shopee Beauty Brand Ads, designed to provide key stakeholders with clear visibility into campaign efficiency and profitability. The analysis showcases over 5.85 billion impressions and 2.4 million clicks, leading to RM1.19 million in total revenue at a highly efficient RM0.05 CPC and RM0.20 CPM. With a 10.42 Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) and RM1.08 million in net profit, the campaign delivered strong financial returns.

Beyond topline success, the dashboard provides actionable insights into user engagement and behavior, such as a 1.80% conversion rate, a modest 0.32% ATC rate, and an average order value of RM27.72. These metrics help uncover optimization opportunities in cart conversions and customer journey flow. This project demonstrates a full-funnel performance marketing analysis ‚Äî integrating paid media metrics with e-commerce KPIs ‚Äî and highlights areas for future improvements across creative, product bundling, and targeting strategies.

Built using Power BI, this project reflects my ability to interpret digital performance data, create business-relevant dashboards, and translate raw numbers into meaningful marketing insight

## üéØ Objective

The objective of this dashboard is to evaluate and optimize the performance of Shopee Beauty Brand paid campaigns by integrating platform advertising metrics with e-commerce sales data. As a media buyer, my focus is to ensure budget efficiency, maximize Return on Ad Spend (ROAS), and uncover opportunities to improve audience targeting, ad creatives, and product positioning.

By visualizing key KPIs such as impressions, clicks, CPC, CPM, conversion rate, and net profit, this dashboard enables data-driven decision-making that supports both short-term performance gains and long-term brand growth.

üìå Stakeholder Use Cases
Marketing Director: Quickly assess campaign ROI, identify top-performing SKUs, and decide where to allocate additional budget for maximum impact.

Brand Manager: Understand which products and creatives resonate most with target audiences to refine messaging and positioning.

E-commerce Manager: Monitor SKU-level sales performance to optimize inventory planning and product visibility on the platform.

Finance & Strategy Teams: Align advertising investment with profitability goals by tracking net profit and ROAS in real time.

This dashboard serves as a practical example of how performance marketing insights can be transformed into actionable strategies that drive profitability in competitive e-commerce environments.

1. Campaign Reach & Engagement
The campaign reached an impressive 5.85 billion impressions, indicating strong visibility across Shopee‚Äôs platform.

This exposure translated to 2.4 million clicks, resulting in a Click-Through Rate (CTR) of 0.41%. While this CTR is modest, it's within range for high-impression awareness campaigns, particularly in the beauty category, where ad fatigue and visual saturation are common challenges.

üìå 2. Cost Efficiency & Ad Spend Health
The total cost of the campaign amounted to RM114,997.65, yielding:

A Cost Per Click (CPC) of RM0.05 ‚Äî indicating highly efficient spend to drive user interaction.

A Cost Per Thousand Impressions (CPM) of RM0.20, which is exceptionally low. This suggests the campaign was well-optimized for cost-efficient reach, ideal for awareness-focused objectives.

The Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) is 10.42, meaning that for every RM1 spent, the campaign generated RM10.42 in revenue ‚Äî an excellent performance benchmark, signaling both high effectiveness and profitability.

üìå 3. Revenue Contribution & Conversion Health
The campaign drove a total Revenue of RM1,197,834.46, with RM1.59M in Order Value and 43,214 Orders.

The Aggregated Order Value stands at RM27.72, meaning that each customer on average spends around RM28 per transaction ‚Äî aligning with typical consumer behavior for mid-tier beauty brands.

The Conversion Rate (Orders/Clicks) is 1.80%, which is slightly below e-commerce benchmarks (usually between 2%-3%) but still acceptable for traffic-oriented campaigns. This might suggest a need to investigate landing page experience or SKU attractiveness.

üìå 4. Funnel Health: Add-To-Cart & Purchase Behavior
The Add to Cart (ATC) metric stands at 7,741, resulting in an ATC Rate of 0.32%. This is relatively low compared to the volume of clicks, indicating potential friction between click intent and purchase consideration.

Despite strong clicks and impressions, the cart-to-order ratio appears steep, which may indicate:

Pricing mismatch

Uncompetitive SKU bundles

Issues with promotional messaging, discounting strategy, or delivery fees at checkout

üìå 5. Net Profit & Campaign Profitability
With a total revenue of RM1.19M and a cost of RM114.9K, the net profit is RM1,082,837 ‚Äî a strong indicator that the media investment was both strategic and efficient.

The profit-to-cost ratio exceeds 9:1, showcasing clear room to scale the campaign or reinvest budget into higher-performing SKUs or categories

2. High Spend vs. Low Efficiency

‚Ä¢	Suggests potential overspending without proportional returns ‚Äî optimization or targeting improvements needed.
3. Revenue vs. ROAS Correlation
‚Ä¢	Crystallure and Kahf both have relatively lower spend yet strong ROAS (16 and 8 respectively), showing efficiency in converting budget into sales.
‚Ä¢	Wardah generates good revenue (RM0.25M) but with a low ROAS, indicating high volume but costly conversions.
4. Impressions Efficiency
‚Ä¢	Crystallure again stands out ‚Äî lower impressions (94M) but high ROAS, implying highly targeted audience reach.
‚Ä¢	Instaperfect+ has the highest impressions (108M) but does not achieve a superior ROAS, hinting at low-quality traffic.
5. Conversion Rates
‚Ä¢	Highest Rate of Conversion: Crystallure (2.06%) and Kahf (1.74%) ‚Äî both are efficient spenders.
‚Ä¢	Lowest: Emina (1.29%), despite mid-range spend and impressions, suggesting a product-market mismatch or weak ad creatives.
6. Clicks & Engagement
‚Ä¢	Crystallure and Kahf show higher CTR efficiency ‚Äî fewer clicks wasted as they translate to higher conversions.
‚Ä¢	Wardah and Emina have clicks but low conversion rates, indicating a drop-off post-click (likely due to landing page or offer issues).

‚Ä¢	Wardah sees higher Add to Cart (2.0K) but weaker conversion ‚Äî possibly cart abandonment issues.
